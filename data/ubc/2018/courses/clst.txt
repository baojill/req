code: CLST 204

cred: 3
desc: A survey of the material cultures of the pre-classical and classical civilizations of Greece and Rome, illustrating the principles and techniques used to illuminate the archaeological history of these civilizations.

code: CLST 231

cred: 3

code: CLST 232
name: Ancient Rome
cred: 3

code: CLST 260
name: Gladiators, Games, and Spectacle in the Greek and Roman World
cred: 3
desc: History, development, and social function of various forms of spectacle in ancient Greece and Rome, from the Olympic games to the Roman arena. 

code: CLST 301
name: The Technical Terms of Medicine and Biological Science
cred: 3
desc: Acquaints the student with the Greek and Latin elements from which most specialized terms of modern medicine are constructed. Intended primarily for students planning to enter the medical, pharmaceutical, or biological sciences. 

code: CLST 306
name: Applied Science and Technology in Classical Antiquity
cred: 3
desc: The origins and achievements of applied technology in the Greek and Roman world from the Bronze Age to late Antiquity, with special attention to archaeological evidence. 

code: CLST 307
name: Greek Law
cred: 3
desc: The study of Greek legal theory, practice, and institutions from their origin in self-help, through the early lawgivers and their codes, to the developed system of Athens in the fifth and fourth centuries. A variety of test cases from the works of the Greek orators will be explored. 

code: CLST 308
name: Roman Law
cred: 3
desc: The development of Roman private law during the classical period with special attention to family law, contract and delict. 

code: CLST 311
name: Women in the Bronze Age, Classical Greek and Hellenistic Cultures
cred: 3
desc: The images projected in mythology, literature, and art are compared with realities of women's lives insofar as they can be reconstructed from historical, legal, and archaeological records. 

code: CLST 312
name: Women in the Roman World of Republican and Imperial Times
cred: 3
desc: Women  in the Roman world in the culture of the Republic and the Empire. Literary, artistic, and mythological sources are compared and contrasted to historical, legal, and archaeological records.
