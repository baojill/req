

code: RELG 101
name: Introduction to the Western (Abrahamic) Religions: Judaism, Christianity, and Islam
cred: 3
desc: An overview of the three main western monotheistic (Abrahamic) religions, together with the concepts used in studying religion, The focus will be on the origins and representative texts along with some historical development and current experience of each religion.

code: RELG 201
name: Near Eastern and Biblical Mythology
cred: 3
desc: An introduction to the world of Near Eastern mythology, from the Gilgamesh Epic to the Book of Genesis and beyond. 

code: RELG 203
name: Scriptures of the Near East
cred: 3
desc: An introduction to the scriptures of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

name: Introduction to Asian Religions

code: RELG 206
name: Introduction to Judaism and its Texts
cred: 3
desc: What is Judaism? An overview of the key texts that have defined the Jewish religion, from the Hebrew Bible through works of contemporary thinkers. Recommended as a basis for upper-level courses in religious studies. 

code: RELG 207
name: Classical Islam
cred: 3
desc: The history and culture, values, and achievements of Islamic societies from 700-1500; the interconnections between power, politics, gender, and the arts in Islamic societies. This course is highly recommended as a basis for all 300- and 400-level Islamic Studies courses. 

code: RELG 208
name: Modern Islam
cred: 3
desc: The history and culture, values, and socio-political movements of the Islamic world from 1500 to the modern day; the interconnections between power, politics, gender, and the arts in modern Islamic societies. This course is highly recommended as a basis for all 300- and 400-level Islamic Studies courses. 

code: RELG 209
name: Eden to Exile: Introduction to the Hebrew Bible
cred: 3
desc: An overview of the Hebrew Bible ("Old Testament"), with emphasis on its ancient Near Eastern context; its competing religious perspectives; and its limits as a historical source. 

code: RELG 210
name: History of Christianity I (1st C. C.E. - 14th C. C.E.)
cred: 3
desc: An introduction to the history of Christianity from the post-Biblical period to the colonial expansion of Christianity under Spain and Portugal. 

code: RELG 211
name: History of Christianity II (15th C. C.E. - present)
cred: 3
desc: The history of Christianity from the Reformation to the present day. Provides an historical overview of Christian thought and its institutions and significant people during the rise of the Reformation in various locales of Europe and beyond (Germany, France, Great Britain, Switzerland, and Scandinavia). 

code: RELG 302
name: Death and Afterlife in Western Religious Tradition
cred: 3
desc: A survey of traditional attitudes toward death and beliefs regarding human existence after death in Western religions. 

code: RELG 305
name: The Prophetic Personality in Ancient Israel
cred: 3
desc: An exploration of the unique phenomenon of the biblical prophet in its wider Near Eastern context. 

code: RELG 306
name: Archaeology and the Bible
cred: 3
desc: The impact of archaeological research on understanding the history and religion of ancient Israel. 

code: RELG 307
name: Sex, Lies, and Violence in the Hebrew Bible
cred: 3
desc: An exploration of the Bible's "dark side," with emphasis on texts that center on sex, deceit, and murder. 

code: RELG 308
name: Talmud, Midrash and the Rabbinic Imagination
cred: 3
desc: The Talmud, Midrash, and other late-antique (3rd - 7th centuries) rabbinic writings, focusing on biblical interpretation and narratives. 

code: RELG 309
name: Jews and Christians
cred: 3
desc: Aspects of Jewish-Christian relations from the beginnings of Christianity to the present day. Emphasis on the study of Christian and Jewish texts in translation. 

code: RELG 310
name: Jewish Responses to Catastrophe
cred: 3
desc: Topics include the destruction of the Second Temple, the Crusades, the expulsions from Spain and Portugal, and the Holocaust. A study of texts in translation. 

code: RELG 311
name: Jewish Literature in Translation
cred: 3
desc: A survey of Jewish literature in medieval and modern times. 

code: RELG 312
name: Jews and Judaism in Canada
cred: 3
desc: The history of the Jewish community in Canada, from New France to the present. Emphasis on the relationship between immigration and religious transformation, Jewish-Christian relations, the development of community structures. 

code: RELG 313
name: Modern Jewish Ethics in Historical Perspective
cred: 3
desc: Readings from key texts in English translation, tracing how Jewish approaches to ethical issues evolved from biblical times to the present. 

code: RELG 315
name: History of Christian Thought
cred: 6
desc: Selected topics with special emphasis on doctrinal change and development, orthodoxy and heresy, tradition and authority, and Church and State in the Patristic, Medieval, Reformation, and Modern periods. 

code: RELG 316
name: The Origins of Christianity: Literary Contexts
cred: 3
desc: The origins of Christianity as reflected in the New Testament and contemporaneous literature. 

code: RELG 317
name: The Origins of Christianity: Social, Religious, and Political Milieux
cred: 3
desc: The origins of Christianity as reflected in early Christian literature of the first and early second centuries (including the New Testament). 

code: RELG 321
name: Prophetic Figures in the Christian Tradition
cred: 3, 6
desc: Examined in their historical context and in terms of their continuing significance. Selection will vary from year to year, but may include Augustine, Thomas Aquinas, Martin Luther, and Teresa of Avila. 

code: RELG 323
name: Christianity in the Modern World
cred: 6
desc: The interaction between Christianity and the major intellectual, social, and cultural developments since 1648 with special attention to the expansion of Christianity and its encounter with urban industrial society. 

code: RELG 328
name: Medieval Philosophy
cred: 3
desc: Survey of Western European thought from Augustine to the fourteenth century. Possible topics and authors include: Augustine; Abelard; the influence of Islam; the rediscovery of Aristotle; Aquinas; Scotus; Ockham. 

code: RELG 330
name: The Origins of Judaism
cred: 3
desc: Surveys the history of Jews and Judaism in the Second Temple era, from the destruction of the First Jerusalem Temple (586 B.C.E.) to the beginnings of the rabbinic movement (200 C.E.). 

code: RELG 331
name: Medieval Jewish History
cred: 3
desc: The political, social, economic, and cultural history of the Jews from the time of the Christianization of the Roman Empire to the expulsion of professing Jews from Spain and Portugal at the end of the fifteenth century. 

code: RELG 332
name: Modern Jewish History
cred: 3
desc: The political, social, economic, and cultural history of the Jews from the beginning of the sixteenth century to the present, with special emphasis on changing attitudes to Jews and Judaism, and social and cultural transformations. 

code: RELG 335
name: Jewish Law
cred: 3
desc: History, sources, theoretical issues and current state of research about early Jewish legal traditions, focusing on close readings of classic primary texts in English translations. 

code: RELG 341
name: Art and Architecture in the Islamic World
cred: 3, 6

code: RELG 365

code: RELG 366
name: Buddhism in China
cred: 3

code: RELG 370

code: RELG 385

code: RELG 414

code: RELG 415
name: The Life and Thought of Paul of Tarsus
cred: 3

code: RELG 420
name: Religion in Canada
cred: 6
desc: An examination of Canadian religious development with special reference to the separation of church and state, the rise of denominationalism and religious pluralism, secularization and ecumenicity, and the emergence of new religious movements. 

code: RELG 448
name: Seminar in the History of the Religion of Islam
cred: 3
desc: A topic relevant to the study of Islam as a religion: e.g., the text and doctrines of the Qur'an; the Hadith (or Traditions) of the Prophet; Islamic Law; mysticism in Islam; the Shi'ah and the Isma'ilis. Not offered every year. Consult the departmental brochure for the topic to be offered. 

code: RELG 452

Those with the necessary preparation may read some texts in the original languages.
